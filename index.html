Kinjal Parikh

I am a first year PhD student at Dynamic Graphics Project lab at the University of Toronto, where I am supervised by Prof. David I.W. Levin. My research area is computer graphics and I am particularly interested in geometry processing. My current focus is on computing shape descriptors for analysis of 3D geometry. To this end, I am working on computing higher-order derivatives through the application of multilinear algebra.

Before starting my PhD I worked at Walmart Global Technology, India as a Software Engineer for a year. I have also worked on Compiler Generation with Prof Uday Khedker at IIT Bombay. I am a part of the first class of Summer Geometry Institute fellows.
